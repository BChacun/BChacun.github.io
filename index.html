QuantLib - Black & Scholes Constant Process

Third year project at IMT Atlantique / BAC + 5

2023

IMT Atlantique, Brest

The goal of this project was to add the possibility of using a black & scholes process with constant parameters to speed up the calculation of NPVs for European, barrier and Asian options in the QuantLib library. This is obviously done at the expense of precision, which is why the addition of a Boolean variable has been added to activate or not this calculation method. The project was therefore done in C++, the language of the library, and evaluated by Mr. Ballabio, the creator of the library, in the context of a teaching unit on market finance.

Human 2 Anime 2 Human

Third year project at IMT Atlantique / BAC + 5

2022

IMT Atlantique, Brest

We studied CycleGans and used this approach to try to create a bijection between human faces and Japanese animation faces. The real data is on the odd columns while the created data is on the even columns.

The main problem of our project comes from the datasets used. Our human portrait dataset is very diverse, we find all origins and some people wear headgear. While our second animation dataset contains almost only female characters of white skin color. A bijection is then very difficult to create.

Added to this is the fact that the model was trained over around 6 hours, for around 2 million parameters and around 5,000 images for each set.

We believe that this mainly explains the lack of quality of our results.

Nuclear Power Plant Model

First year project at IMT Atlantique / BAC + 3

2020 - 2021

IMT Atlantique, Nantes

During this project, we produced a model of a nuclear power plant.

We did some research to find a way to model the core and came up with an equation.
The core was then coded on MatLab to which we added an automatic bar power management algorithm using a solver on our equation.
Thanks to it, we control the valve of a vaporette with a theoretical sinking of the control uranium bars.

This device simulates the steam production of the plant.
We then use this steam to produce electricity, thanks to a steam engine and to power a lamp.
